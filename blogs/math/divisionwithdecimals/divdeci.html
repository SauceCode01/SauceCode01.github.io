A decimal number is a number that is composed of two parts separated by a decimal point. The whole number part and the fractional part.

Division with decimal is not very different with division with integers except that there is a decimal point.

How to do division with decimals?

To make it easier to follow, we divided the lesson into three parts:

  1. whole number divided by a decimal.
  2. decimal divided by a whole number.
  3. decimal divided by a decimal.

Decimal divided by a whole number

Question: What is 24 divided by 4.8?

Write down the dividend and the divisor in the format below.

Place a decimal point directly above the decimal point of the dividend.

Do long division like normal.

Whole number divided by a decimal

Question: what is 7.83 divided by 3?

Write down the dividend and the divisor in the format below.

Move the decimal point of the divisor until it become a whole number. Count the number of steps the decimal point has to take to be a whole number.

Put a zero to the dividend depending on the number of steps you counted. If you counted 2 steps, then put 2 zeros at the end of the dividend.

If the dividend is still a decimal, go back to the first case where we are dividing a decimal with a whole number.

Decimal divided by a decimal

Question: What is 6.25 divided by 0.25?

Write down the dividend and the divisor in the format below.

Move the decimal point of the divisor until it become a whole number. Count the number of steps the decimal point has to take to be a whole number.

Move the decimal point of the divisor to the right the same number of steps that you counted.

Do the steps in the first case where we divide a whole number by a decimal.

Example questios

1. What is 2.5 divided by 13.25

This problem falls under case number 3. Decimal divided by a decimal.

Arrange the divisor and the dividend.

Move the decimal point of the divisor to the right until the divisor became a whole number.

The decimal point needed two steps so that the divisor is a whole number. Move the decimal point of the dividend two steps to the right.

Do normal division.
