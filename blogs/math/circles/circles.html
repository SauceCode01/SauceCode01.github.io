What is a circle?

Circle is a set of points equidistant to a fixed point called the center.
The parts of a circle includes the center, circumference, chord, diameter, radius, tangent, arc, segment, and sector.

Center

Center is a point in the circle that is equidistant to every point on the circle. The distance from the center to a point on the circle is called the radius.

Example: If the point (3, 5) lies on a circle whose center is at (0, 0), what is the radius?

Circumference

Circumference is the distance around the circle. It is like the perimeter which is the distance around a polygon. The cricumference is where the points equidistant to the center lies. To find the circumference, you just multiply the diameter by pi or 2 radius times pi.

Chord

A chord is a line segment connecting two points on a curve. In a circle, a chord is a line segment whose endpoints lies on the circle.

Diameter

The diameter is a chord(the longest chord) that passes through the center. All diameter of the same or two congruent circles are congruent.

Radius

The distance from the center to a point on the circle is called radius. A radius is a line segment connecting the center to a point on the circle. The length of a radius is half the length of the diameter. All radii of the same or two congruent circles are congruent.

Tangent

A tangent is a line that only intersect with the circle once. The point where the tangent intersected with the circle is called the point of tangency. It is always perpendicular to the radius.

Arc

An arc is a portion of the circumference.

Segment

A segment is a region in the circle enclosed by a chord an an arc.

Sector

A sector is a region in the circle enclosed by two radius and an arc.
